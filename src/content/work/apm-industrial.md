### 01 — Context

PT Artha Prima Mekanika manufactures downhole packer and anchor equipment for oil & gas operations across Indonesia and Southeast Asia. With 20 years of production history and over 200 SKUs across seven product lines, their digital presence had not kept up: product data was scattered across PDFs and sales decks, and there was no central place for engineers or procurement teams to browse specifications.

### 02 — Problem

The immediate need was a credible public-facing website. The deeper problem was catalog access: their sales team was the bottleneck between a client asking "what fits a 7-inch casing?" and getting an answer. The site needed to remove that friction without becoming an e-commerce platform, which is not appropriate for a B2B industrial context.

### 03 — Approach

Structure first, aesthetics second. Industrial clients expect authority and clarity, not polish. The information hierarchy was mapped from the procurement workflow: company context, then product categories, then individual specs, then a direct line to the sales team. The inquiry-only model (no prices, no cart, just a phone number) was a deliberate constraint, not a limitation.

### 04 — Execution

Two self-contained pages, no framework dependencies. The landing page covers brand context, product categories, and key capabilities in a single vertical scroll. The catalog page hosts all 38 products across seven categories with a JS-driven filter and URL query sync, so links to specific categories work and can be shared directly. All product visuals use CSS-only placeholder art.

### 05 — Results

The site handles the full browsing journey from category to specification without a single page reload. The inquiry path is three actions: browse, read spec, call.

- **7** product categories
- **38** products with full specification lines
- **0** external dependencies beyond Google Fonts
