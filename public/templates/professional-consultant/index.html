§ 01 — The firm
About Halden & Meridian

A small firm by design, not by stage.

Founded in 1998 by a tax partner and a corporate lawyer who had grown weary of the leverage model. We've kept the firm intentionally narrow: twenty-four professionals, four partners, one office floor.

"We are not interested in being the largest firm in the room. We are interested in being the one the principals ask to stay behind after everyone else has left."

— J. Halden
Founding partner · Tax & restructuring

Our clients reach us at the points where a matter has stopped being procedural and started being consequential — a transfer-pricing audit headed for assessment, a shareholder dispute drifting toward filing, a regulator that has begun asking pointed questions instead of routine ones.

We staff lean. Every engagement is partner-led, every memo is partner-signed, and every consequential conversation involves a name on the door. We do not push files down a hierarchy; the principal you meet at the first consultation is the principal you brief on the last.

We hold practice rights in Indonesia, Singapore, and the Netherlands, and work alongside referred counsel in fifteen further jurisdictions. We accept roughly one in four engagements offered to us; the others we decline, refer, or politely return.
